About Me

Jim Saetern has spent his personal and career life working in a variety of fields. Before focusing on work in the software development field, Jim has worked in jobs that range from a cook to a mechanical engineer. This experience has allowed him to have a unique perspective and has aided him in bring unique solutions and inputs to solving client needs. His time as a software developer has also led him to work in different roles, giving him experience in areas such as but not limited to a database administrator, fullstack developer, mobile developer, and database architect.
Because of the his experiences, Jim is always pursuing knowledge in new fields and has a flexable mindset in order to work with different people. He knows that there is no single approach that works for every solutions and so continues to educated himself on emerging technologies.

He is an avid hiker and nature enthusiast, spending much of his free time experiencing and drawing inspiration from the wonders of the natural world. Drawing from nature, Jim tackles each challenge presented to him in very much the same way as he would during a hike. He believes that all work, no matter how insignificant it may seem, will always lead to the goal much like each step on a trail leads to the top of the mountain.

He received his education at the California State University, Sacramento with a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science. Jim is a former member of the Japanese and Hmong Health Alliance clubs during his stay at university, in which he was an administrative member for the latter.
